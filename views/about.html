About

The web is my canvas, the computer is my medium.

I am hesitant to try new foods and cringe at the thought of playing a rough sport, but I am fearless when it comes to software engineering. I am eager to learn new technologies and challenge my thinking—even if that technology looks green and unfamiliar like basil on a pizza. Additionally, I am not afraid to fail. Although I may get hit by “dodge balls” throughout the learning process, I view failure as an opportunity to grow.

Here’s a little peak into my educational and professional journey so far:

  • I am currently a software engineer at Demandware in Burlington, MA working mainly in Angular to develop their cloud e-commerce platform.
  • I graduated summa cum laude with a bachelor of science in computer science and a minor in business administration in May 2015. I still have a lot to learn, but I completed my studies with an ability to reason, solve, and persist.
  • I belonged to a university research lab, the Engaging Computing Group, for 3 years of my undergraduate career on the iSENSE Project. I joined in 2012 as a QA engineer and later settled into release management. I served as a bridge between many teams—web, mobile, API, and educational. I embraced both the technical and communicative aspects of this role, and I got really good at source control.
  • I spent 4 months of summer 2014 working at a tiny powerhouse, User Interface Engineering (UIE), in North Andover, MA as a front-end web developer intern. My responsibilities were mainly technical—building web pages, working with the MailChimp API, as well as writing and executing QA test plans—but I was immersed in the world of design simply by “osmosis.” I worked alongside brilliant UX experts including Jared Spool.
  • I continued my relationship with the wonderful folks at UIE throughout my senior year of college as a QA contract engineer. I wrote and executed more QA test plans to help the company in sustaining their bug-free and delightful conference websites for many years to come.

My long-term dream goes beyond technology.

My long-term dream is to work at the intersection of technology and leadership. Although I like working hands-on with technology as an engineer, I believe that my true bliss (and greatest strength) is being a leader.

I think there is something to be said about working in an environment where people are encouraged to excel, embrace learning, and, most importantly, enjoy coming to every day. Simply put, I aspire to be in a position to lead people in doing great things while loving and thriving every second of it.
